Public Transportation Guide - Daegu

1. Subway

Daegu has a well-connected subway system with three lines (Line 1, Line 2, and Line 3). You can buy a transportation card (T-money) at any subway station or convenience store, which can be used for subways, buses, and taxis. The subway operates from approximately 5:30 AM to midnight.

2. Bus

Daegu's bus system covers a wide range of routes. Buses are color-coded based on their routes: green, red, yellow, and blue. You can use your T-money card on buses as well. Bus stops have route maps, and you can check bus arrival times via mobile apps such as 'Daegu Bus'.

3. Taxi

Taxis in Daegu are readily available and can be hailed on the street or through mobile apps. Most taxis accept T-money cards or cash. While taxi drivers may not always speak English, having your destination written in Korean can be very helpful.

4. Transfer Between Subway and Bus

In Daegu, you can transfer between the subway and bus for free within 30 minutes of tapping out at a subway station or bus stop (up to four transfers). Simply use the same T-money card for both services. Transfers must occur between the same modes of transportation (subway-bus or bus-bus).

5. Recommended Apps

We recommend downloading the following apps to help with navigating Daegu's public transportation:

  • Daegu Bus: Provides real-time bus arrival times and route maps.
  • Subway Korea: Offers subway maps and real-time updates for Daegu's subway system.
  • Kakao T: A popular app for calling taxis and checking public transportation routes.
