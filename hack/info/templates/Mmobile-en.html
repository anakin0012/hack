Mobile Service Guide

1. Choose a Mobile Carrier

In South Korea, there are three major mobile carriers: SK Telecom, KT, and LG U+. Each carrier offers various plans for both prepaid and postpaid services. Visit a nearby store for these carriers to explore their options.

2. Required Documents

When signing up for a mobile plan, make sure you have the following documents:

  • Alien Registration Card (ARC)
  • Passport
  • Bank account information (for postpaid plans)

3. Prepaid vs. Postpaid

Prepaid: You pay in advance for the usage, and you can top up your balance as needed. Prepaid plans are popular for short-term stays.

Postpaid: You sign a contract and pay monthly based on your usage. Postpaid plans usually require a bank account for automatic payments.

4. Mobile Apps for Easy Management

Each carrier offers mobile apps that help you manage your plan, check your data usage, and pay your bills. You can download apps such as:

  • SK Telecom: T World
  • KT: KT Customer
  • LG U+: U+ 고객센터
